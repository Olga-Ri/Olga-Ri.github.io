Who I am

As a data analyst and cognitive science student at Barnard College, I’m driven by a belief that data can be a force for equity. My work focuses on uncovering systemic disparities — like food insecurity in NYC or gaps in educational access — to advocate for marginalized communities. My Latinx heritage fuels my commitment to amplifying underrepresented voices through data storytelling.

What I Do

At Columbia’s Data Science Institute, I dig into socioeconomic data to uncover the challenges underserved New Yorkers face. With Python and Excel, I clean, analyze, and visualize data—helping nonprofits make smarter decisions about where to direct resources. So far, my work has shaped real-world interventions in three high-need neighborhoods.

My Vision

I believe data can be a force for equity—not just highlighting systemic gaps, but actively helping to close them. My work focuses on partnering with community organizations and policymakers to transform insights into programs that expand access to healthcare, education, and economic opportunity. Data shouldn’t just describe inequality; it should help dismantle it. By centering the voices of marginalized communities in my analysis, I aim to create meaningful change where it matters most.
